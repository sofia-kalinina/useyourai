Privacy Policy

Effective date: 26 April 2026  ·  Last updated: 26 April 2026

1. Who is responsible for your data

useyourai ("we", "us") is the data controller for personal data collected through useyourai.eu. For any privacy-related questions or requests, contact us at: privacy@useyourai.eu.

2. What data we collect and why

Data Purpose Legal basis
Email address Create and identify your account; send account confirmation and password-reset codes Contract (providing the service you signed up for)
Exercise prompts (free text you type, ≤ 500 characters) Generate language exercises via AI Contract
Exercise answers (free text you type, ≤ 300 characters) Evaluate your answers and generate feedback via AI Contract
Session metadata (topic, language, level, feedback preference) Personalise exercises and feedback to your chosen settings Contract
Internal user identifier (a random UUID assigned at sign-up) Link your sessions to your account; enforce access control Legitimate interest (security, data integrity)

We do not collect your name, phone number, payment details, or any data beyond what is listed above. We do not use cookies for tracking or advertising. We do not embed any third-party analytics scripts.

3. How long we keep your data

  • Exercise prompts, answers, and session data — automatically deleted after 24 hours via a database TTL (time-to-live) mechanism. No manual action required on your part.
  • Account data (email address) — retained for as long as your account exists. To request account deletion, email privacy@useyourai.eu.

4. Who processes your data (sub-processors)

We use Amazon Web Services (AWS) as our cloud infrastructure provider, covered by AWS's standard Data Processing Addendum.

Service Region What it handles
Amazon Cognito eu-central-1 (Frankfurt) Account authentication — stores your email address and hashed password
Amazon DynamoDB eu-central-1 (Frankfurt) Session storage — holds exercise prompts, answers, and session metadata (deleted after 24 h)
AWS Lambda & API Gateway eu-central-1 (Frankfurt) Application logic — processes requests in-transit; no persistent storage
Amazon S3 & CloudFront eu-central-1 / global edge Hosts and delivers the website front end (static files only; no user data)
Amazon Bedrock (Claude Sonnet) eu-central-1 (Frankfurt) AI model invocation — your prompts and answers are sent here for exercise generation and evaluation
AI model and your data: We use Claude via Amazon Bedrock. AWS's policy for Bedrock is that customer inputs and outputs are not used to train or improve foundation models. Your prompts and answers are processed transiently to generate a response and are not retained by the model provider beyond that request. See the AWS Bedrock FAQ for details.

We do not share your personal data with any other third parties.

5. Browser storage

When you sign in, our authentication library stores your session tokens in your browser's localStorage. These tokens allow you to stay signed in without re-entering your password on every visit. The access token expires after 1 hour; the refresh token after 30 days. Clearing your browser's site data removes them immediately.

We do not use tracking or advertising cookies. No cookie consent banner is shown because the only browser storage we use is strictly necessary to provide the service you signed in to use.

6. Your rights

Under GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your account and all associated data.
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Restriction — ask us to limit processing while a dispute is resolved.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@useyourai.eu. We will respond within 30 days.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority. In Germany: Bundesbeauftragte für den Datenschutz (BfDI).

7. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we will update the effective date at the top of this page. Continued use of the service after a change constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
